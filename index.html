Who Owns This Property?

Decide the dispute. Understand the law.

Story

A buys land in 2000.

B starts using the land quietly.

15 years pass...

A returns and claims ownership.

Who owns the property?

Correct Answer: B

Under the Limitation Act, continuous and hostile possession for 12+ years may extinguish the original owner's title.

Is this fair?

The law prioritizes certainty, but may reward wrongful possession.

Practice

A occupies land for only 5 years. Can ownership shift?

No. The statutory period (12 years) is not satisfied.



Story 2: Too Late to Claim

A owns land.

B forcefully takes possession.

13 years pass. A goes to court.

The court dismisses the case as time-barred.

Can A recover the land?

Correct Answer: No

The claim is barred by limitation. After 12 years, the owner loses the right to recover the property.

Important: This does not automatically make B the owner. A loses the legal remedy.



Story 3: Permission vs Possession

A allows B to use the land with permission.

10 years pass. B continues using the land.

B now claims: “This is my land.”

The dispute reaches court.

Does B become the owner?

Correct Answer: No

Adverse possession requires hostile possession. If use began with permission, it cannot become adverse unless clearly denied.

Key Idea: Permission destroys hostility—so no adverse possession.
